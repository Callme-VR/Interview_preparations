## 1. Introduction & Tone
**Target Audience:** Fresh graduates (0–1 year experience).
**Tone:** Professional, encouraging, and structured.
**Objective:** To identify foundational knowledge, learnability, and cultural alignment rather than deep industrial expertise.

---

## 2. About the Role & Company Fit
*Focus: Alignment with mission, research, and career goals.*

| # | Question | Model Answer / Scoring Cues |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"Why did you apply to this company specifically?"** | Look for: Specific mention of products, tech stack, or company values. Red flag: Generic answer that fits any company. |
| 2 | **"What part of the Job Description excited you the most?"** | Look for: Connection between their projects/internships and the role’s requirements. |
| 3 | **"Where do you see your technical skills evolving in 2 years?"** | Look for: Realistic growth (e.g., "Mastering backend," "Learning AWS") vs. vague "Manager" roles. |
| 4 | **"How do you stay updated with tech trends?"** | Look for: Blogs, GitHub, newsletters, or side projects. |
| 5 | **"What interests you most about the industry we operate in (e.g., Fintech, EdTech)?"** | Look for: Industry-specific knowledge or genuine curiosity about the domain. |
| 6 | **"If you could change one thing about our current product/website, what would it be?"** | Look for: Critical thinking and user-centric mindset. |

## 3. Technical & Problem-Solving (Entry-Level)
*Focus: Fundamental concepts and logic over syntax.*

### A. Basic Programming Concepts
*   **Q1: Explain OOP Principles (Abstraction, Encapsulation, Inheritance, Polymorphism) with a real-world example.**
    *   *Rubric:* 3 pts for clear real-world analogy (e.g., a Car or Smartphone); 1 pt for just naming them.
*   **Q2: What is the difference between a Stack and a Queue?**
    *   *Rubric:* Mentioning LIFO vs. FIFO is essential.
*   **Q3: What is the difference between a Process and a Thread?**
    *   *Rubric:* Look for: Memory sharing, overhead, and execution context.
*   **Q4: Explain the concept of a REST API to a 5-year-old.**
    *   *Rubric:* Look for: Ability to simplify (e.g., a waiter in a restaurant).

### B. Simple Coding Approach
*   **Q5: How would you find the first non-repeating character in a string?**
    *   *Cues:* Look for use of HashMaps or frequency arrays. Ask about time complexity (O(n)).
*   **Q6: Write a function to check if a word is a palindrome.**
    *   *Cues:* Check for: Loop efficiency (O(n/2)) or string reversal.

### C. Debugging & Scenarios
*   **Q7: "The code works on your local machine but fails in the testing environment. What are your first three steps?"**
    *   *Cues:* 1. Check environment variables; 2. Compare data/database states; 3. Check logs.
*   **Q8: "You are given a legacy code file with no documentation. How do you start understanding it?"**
    *   *Cues:* Run the code, add print statements/logs, use a debugger, trace the entry point.
*   **Q9: "A user reports the app is 'slow.' How do you begin investigating the bottleneck?"**
    *   *Cues:* Check network latency, database query times, large loops in frontend, or server resources.
*   **Q10: "What is a database index, and how does it improve performance?"**
    *   *Cues:* Compare to a book index; faster reads but slower writes.
*   **Q11: "Explain the difference between Git Merge and Git Rebase."**
    *   *Cues:* Merge keeps history intact with a merge commit; Rebase rewrites history for a linear path.

## 4. Behavioral & Situational Questions
*Framework: STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) or CAR (Context, Action, Result).*

| Question | What to Look For |
|---|---|
| **"Describe a time you had a conflict during a group project."** | Focus on *resolution* and *professionalism* rather than blaming others. |
| **"Tell me about a time you failed to meet a deadline."** | Focus on *communication* (did they tell the team early?) and *learning*. |
| **"Explain a complex technical concept to a non-technical person."** | Ability to simplify without losing the core meaning (Communication skill). |
| **"Describe a time you had to learn a new tool or language in a very short time."** | Look for: Learning strategy (docs, tutorials, practice) and adaptability. |
| **"Give an example of a mistake you made and how you handled it."** | Look for: Ownership, accountability, and the "lesson learned." |

## 5. Role-Specific Variants

### Profile: Software Developer
*   **Q1: What is Version Control (Git) and why is it important?** (Look for: Collaboration, rollbacks).
*   **Q2: Difference between an Interface and an Abstract Class?**
*   **Look for:** Logical flow, clean code mindset, and understanding of the SDLC.

### Profile: QA Tester
*   **Q1: What is the difference between Severity and Priority?** (Look for: Impact on system vs. urgency to fix).
*   **Q2: How do you write a bug report?** (Look for: Steps to reproduce, expected vs. actual results).
*   **Look for:** Attention to detail and "breaking" mindset.

### Profile: Data Analyst
*   **Q1: Explain the difference between an Inner Join and a Left Join.**
*   **Q2: How do you handle missing or "dirty" data in a dataset?** (Look for: Mean/median imputation, removal, or investigation).
*   **Look for:** Analytical rigor and basic SQL/Python knowledge.

## 6. Scoring Guidelines & Rubrics

| Score | Rating | Criteria |
|---|---|---|
| **0** | **Unsatisfactory** | No answer, or answer shows fundamental misunderstanding. Red flags present. |
| **1** | **Developing** | Basic knowledge but needs significant guidance. Theoretical but no practical application. |
| **2** | **Proficient** | Clear answer with examples. Understands the "Why" and "How." Good logic. |
| **3** | **Exceptional** | Deep understanding, relates to real-world impact, high learnability. |

**Red Flags:**
- Blaming team members for failures.
- Claiming to know everything (lack of humility).
- Unable to explain their own project on their resume.

## 7. Interview Logistics & Tips

*   **Duration:** 45–60 minutes per round.
*   **Modes:**
    *   *Video:* Ensure camera is on, check lighting, use a quiet space.
    *   *Onsite:* Bring a notepad and copies of your resume.
*   **Handling Tricky Questions:** If you don't know the answer, say: *"I haven't worked with that specific tool yet, but based on my knowledge of [related tool], I would assume it works like..."*
*   **Interviewer Prompts:** "Tell me more about that," "What would you change if you had more time?"

## 9. Candidate Preparation Tips
- **Research the Product:** Use the app or read the documentation before the interview.
- **Mock Interviews:** Practice the STAR method out loud.
- **Fundamentals First:** For freshers, being strong in Data Structures and Algorithms is more important than knowing 10 frameworks.
- **Ask Questions:** Always have 2-3 questions ready for the interviewer (e.g., "What does a typical day look like for a junior dev here?").
